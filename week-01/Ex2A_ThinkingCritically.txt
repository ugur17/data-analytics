- Does higher search interest in Google Trends really mean people actually prefer or consume that food?

3. The article concluded that “milkshakes take the crown as the ultimate breakup food,
topping the list in six states.” Pull up Google Trends data on Milkshake (beverage) and
change the date range to February 1, 2024 through February 28, 2025.

b) What is the lowest dip recorded?

4. At the top next to the box for “Milkshake,” there is a plus sign to add another search
term. Click the plus sign to add a search for Hamburger (Food).

a) What is the highest peak recorded for searches using Hamburger? What is the
lowest dip?

- highest: 100.   lowest: 54

b) What happened to the line for Milkshake? What do the high and low look like now?

- The Milkshake line changed because Google Trends adjusts values relative to the highest search term in the comparison. Since Hamburger has higher search interest, Milkshake appears lower on the scale. Looks like 28 is the highest now, and 17 is the lowest point on the scale of milkshake.

5. Why did the line for Milkshake change? Hint: Click on the icon next to “Interest
over time” for more information about how trend results are displayed.

- The line for Milkshake changed because Google Trends displays data as relative values, not absolute values. When another search term like Hamburger is added, the data is rescaled so that the highest value among all terms becomes 100. As a result, the Milkshake line appears lower because it is now being compared to a more popular search term.

6. Add a third term to your search, this time for Breakup (Topic).

a) Do you see any patterns that suggest a relationship between searches for Breakup
and Hamburger?

- There may be slight similarities at certain points, but there is no strong or consistent pattern. Overall, the relationship between Breakup and Hamburger searches is weak and not clearly connected.

b) How about between Breakup and Milkshake?

- There is no clear pattern that suggests a strong relationship between Breakup and Milkshake searches. The trends appear mostly independent from each other.

7. What does all of this suggest about the reliability of the conclusions reached in the
article?

- This suggests that the conclusions in the article may not be fully reliable. The data is based on relative search interest, which does not necessarily reflect actual behavior or preferences. Additionally, the lack of clear patterns between related topics shows that the conclusions may be oversimplified or misleading.
